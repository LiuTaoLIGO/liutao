Power Coupling Losses for Misaligned and Mode-mismatched Higher-order Hermite–Gauss Modes

Summary: Higher-order HG modes are particularly susceptible against misalignment and mode mismatch induced power coupling losses.

Reference: Liu Tao, Jessica Kelley-Derzon, Anna C. Green, and Paul Fulda, “Power coupling losses for misaligned and mode-mismatched higher-order Hermite–Gauss modes”, Opt. Lett. 46, 2694 (2021).

We have demonstrated in this work that higher-order Hermite-Gauss (HG) modes can be made robust enough against mirror surface figure imperfections. However, Jones and Freise have shown that the mode-mismatch-induced power losses increase monotonically with mode index when, for example, coupling into optical cavities.

This work took an analytical approach and extends their work to include the case of misalignment. We show that higher-order HG modes are more susceptible to beam perturbations when, for example, coupling into optical cavities: the misalignment and mode-mismatch-induced power coupling losses scale linearly and quadratically with respect to the mode indices, respectively.
