Beam Displacement Tolerances on a Segmented Mirror for Higher-order Hermite-Gauss Modes

Summary: Odd-indexed higher-order HG modes are more compatible with segmented mirrors for further noise reduction.

Reference: Liu Tao, Nina Brown and Paul Fulda, “Beam Displacement Tolerances on a Segmented Mirror for Higher-order Hermite-Gauss Modes”, LIGO-P2300055.

Besides the thermal noise benefit of higher-order HG mode, one other potential benefit of using higher-order HG modes is that one can take advantage of their intrinsic intensity pattern symmetries to use larger and more massive segmented mirrors, fabricated from smaller substrates that are limited by the “boule size” of high-purity silicon. This allows a further reduction of thermal noises and quantum radiation pressure noise.

The advantage of odd-indexed HG modes here comes from the fact that the intensity nulls can be aligned with the bond lines, thus avoiding interaction of light with the bonds and the associated scattered light penalties and thermal noise. However, a misplaced HG beam can cause extra power loss due to the bright intensity spots probing the bond lines.

This work analytically and numerically studied the beam displacement tolerances on a segmented mirror for the HG33 mode. We concluded that for “effective” bond lines with 6 µm width, and the HG33 beam size chosen to guarantee 1 ppm clipping loss when centered, the beam can be rotated by roughly 1 degree or laterally displaced by 4 % of its beam size while keeping the total power on the bond lines under 1 ppm. We also demonstrate that the constrained beam displacement parameter region that guarantees a given power loss limit, or the beam displacement tolerance, is inversely proportional to the bond line thickness.
